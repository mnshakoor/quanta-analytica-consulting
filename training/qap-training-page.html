Core Doctrine

Structured reasoning precedes generative output.

QAP does not begin with a conclusion. It begins with a bounded problem, a validated evidence base, and an explicit reasoning structure — before any judgment is formed or any product is written.

The QAP Principle

Frame the problem. Validate the evidence. Structure the reasoning. Assess the risk. Then produce the output.

Every QAP course enforces this sequence. Learners do not jump to conclusions; they build the analytic architecture that makes conclusions defensible. The result is not information — it is structured judgment, proportionate to available evidence, traceable to source, and calibrated to decision need.

Who This Trains

Cross-domain analysts and domain specialists

Phases 1–3 train any analyst, researcher, consultant, risk professional, journalist, or policy analyst. Phases 4–5 train practitioners and organizations operating in MNS Consulting's native domain ecosystem — from NGO security to corporate risk to conflict systems analysis.

What You Produce

Decision-ready analytic products

Every course produces a learner deliverable — a structured analytic brief, risk assessment, scenario product, behavioral profile, or intelligence report. QAP training is output-oriented. The deliverable is the proof of method, not a test grade.

The Method

Six stages. One analytic architecture.

Each phase of the QAP curriculum maps to one or more stages of the core analytic workflow below.

01 — Frame
Problem Framing

Define the problem, decision context, and Key Intelligence Questions before any collection begins.

02 — Collect
Evidence Validation

WSI standards, RAPITIS scoring, C/D/F claim scoring, atomic claim extraction, and source triangulation.

03 — Structure
Analytic Techniques

ACH, Key Assumptions Check, Indicators & Warnings, scenario logic, confidence language, and bias control.

04 — Assess
Risk and Scenarios

CARVER scoring, risk matrices, decision thresholds, Cone of Plausibility, early warning systems, behavioral risk.

05 — Validate
Compliance & Red Team

Source compliance, logic review, red-team critique, assumption checks, confidence gap analysis.

06 — Output
Intelligence Product

BLUF, Key Judgments, SitReps, executive briefs, QAP web reports, and full assessment packages.

01 // Framework Overview

The Quanta Analytica Process™

Core Doctrine

Structured reasoning precedes generative output. The QAP doctrine requires analysts to frame the problem, validate evidence, structure reasoning, and assess risk before producing any analytic, operational, or AI-assisted output.

The result is decision-grade intelligence — not information, not opinion, but structured judgment proportionate to available evidence.

What QAP Trains

  • Problem framing & KIQ development
  • Evidence validation & source discipline
  • Structured analytic techniques
  • Risk scoring & scenario modeling
  • Systems thinking & cognition
  • LLM-governed AI workflows
  • Intelligence writing & briefing
Catalog Scope12 core courses across 5 phases, 8 domain specialization tracks, and 5 certification packages — for individuals, teams, and organizations.

02 // The QAP Method

Six stages from raw information to decision-ready output

01 FrameDefine the problem, decision context, and Key Intelligence Questions.
02 CollectGather, validate, and triangulate evidence using WSI & RAPITIS standards.
03 StructureApply SATs: ACH, Key Assumptions Check, Indicators & Warnings.
04 AssessScore risk, model scenarios, apply behavioral and systems analysis.
05 ValidateCheck assumptions, confidence gaps, source compliance, and red-team logic.
06 OutputProduce a structured, defensible, decision-ready analytic product.
Training principleEach course phase maps to one or more stages of the QAP workflow. Learners build toward full-cycle analyst capability.

"Structured reasoning precedes generative output."

This is the organizing principle of every QAP™ course. Analysts do not begin with a conclusion. They begin with a bounded problem, a validated evidence base, and an explicit reasoning structure — before any judgment is formed or any product is written.

Principle 01 — Frame First

Define the problem before you collect

Every QAP sequence begins with explicit problem framing — bounded analytic problems, Key Intelligence Questions, and collection requirements — before a single source is pulled. Undisciplined collection is the most common source of analytic failure.

Principle 02 — Evidence Discipline

Validate sources before you reason

QAP applies WSI standards, RAPITIS scoring, C/D/F claim scoring, and atomic claim extraction to every evidence base. Source quality is a precondition of judgment quality — not an afterthought.

Principle 03 — Structure the Reasoning

Apply structured analytic techniques before you conclude

ACH, Key Assumptions Check, Indicators and Warnings, and scenario logic are not optional overlays. They are the mechanism by which QAP controls for cognitive bias, mirrors logic errors, and produces defensible judgments under uncertainty.

Principle 04 — Calibrated Output

Confidence language must match evidence strength

Every QAP output carries explicit confidence assessments proportionate to evidence quality, source triangulation, and gap analysis. Intelligence is not assertion — it is structured judgment with traceable provenance.

What is the Quanta Analytica Process™ (QAP™)?

The Quanta Analytica Process™ is a framework-governed analytic methodology that requires structured reasoning before generative output. It trains practitioners to frame problems, validate evidence, apply structured analytic techniques, score risk, model scenarios, govern AI-assisted workflows, and produce decision-ready intelligence products. It was developed by M. Nuri Shakoor under MNS Consulting and ARAC International and is the operating methodology behind Quanta Analytica's intelligence production.

Who is this training program designed for?

Phases 1–3 are designed for any analyst, researcher, consultant, risk professional, journalist, policy analyst, or knowledge worker who needs a structured analytic workflow in their domain — security, governance, business, media, policy, humanitarian, or corporate intelligence. Phases 4–5 are for practitioners and organizations operating in MNS Consulting's native domain ecosystem, including NGO security, corporate risk, conflict systems analysis, insider threat, behavioral risk, information integrity, and strategic communications risk.

How many courses are in the program?

The QAP™ program includes 12 core courses across 5 phases, plus 40+ domain specialization courses across 8 tracks in Phase 5. Each domain track includes 5 courses and a capstone. Five certification packages are available — from the Analyst Foundations Certificate (Phase 1 only) through the Domain Specialization Track (Phase 5).

What does each course produce?

Every QAP course is output-oriented — each produces a learner deliverable. Examples include a Problem Frame and KIQ Set (Course 01), an Evidence Log and Atomic Claim Table (Course 02), a Structured Analytic Judgment Brief (Course 03), a Decision-Ready Risk Assessment (Course 04), a Behavioral Risk Assessment Brief (Course 06), a Strategic Systems Diagnostic Brief (Course 07), a QAP-Governed AI Workflow Pack (Course 08), and a Full QAP Assessment (Course 11). The deliverable is the proof of method.

What is LLM-governed analytic workflow training?

Course 08 — LLM-Governed Analytic Workflows — teaches analysts how to govern AI tools within QAP's analytic architecture. This includes prompt architecture, role-constrained AI personas, hallucination controls, source discipline in AI-assisted collection, and QAP-governed output validation. It is designed for practitioners already using tools like Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Mistral, or NotebookLM who need a disciplined governance framework for AI-augmented analysis.

Does MNS Consulting offer advisory support beyond the courses?

Yes. MNS Consulting provides three types of advisory support: (1) Implementation advisory — helping teams adopt QAP workflows inside existing analytic, risk, security, or reporting processes; (2) Domain adaptation — customizing QAP templates, indicators, source registers, risk matrices, and analytic products for a client's specific sector; (3) Production support — assisting with QAP web reports, dashboards, evidence registers, capstone assessments, audit checklists, and training rollouts. Fee-based engagements are available to both individual practitioners and organizations.

Can QAP be applied outside the listed domain tracks?

Yes. The QAP methodology is domain-transferable. The eight Phase 5 tracks represent domains where MNS Consulting has developed native workflow experience, but the method can be adapted for additional sectors. MNS Consulting can support organizations in configuring QAP evidence needs, indicator sets, risk models, and output templates for domains not yet covered by the standard Phase 5 library, on a fee-based advisory basis.

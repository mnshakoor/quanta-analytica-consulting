02
Positive Peace

The structural lens for systemic resilience

Positive Peace reframes stability as more than the absence of violence. Within the QAP™ framework, it operates as a diagnostic tool for systemic resilience: the ability of a society or operating environment to prevent, absorb, and recover from shocks without structural breakdown.

The core analytic question becomes: why was this system unable to absorb this shock? The answer depends on the condition and interdependence of the underlying pillars that support resilience.<

The 8 pillars of Positive Peace as systems diagnostic steps under the QA-SIA conceptual model

  • Well-Functioning Government: institutional legitimacy and crisis coordination capacity.
  • Sound Business Environment: economic resilience and adaptive private-sector capacity.
  • Equitable Distribution of Resources: buffer against grievance accumulation.
  • Acceptance of the Rights of Others: social cohesion during stress.
  • Good Relations with Neighbors: protection against spillover dynamics.
  • Free Flow of Information: transparency and early-warning viability.
  • High Levels of Human Capital: recovery speed and adaptive capacity.
  • Low Levels of Corruption: institutional integrity under pressure.

Pillar interdependency

Weakness in one pillar can cascade through the system. Corruption undermines legitimacy. Reduced legitimacy degrades collective action. Weakened collective action constrains information flow and crisis response. This is where structural fragility becomes a predictor rather than a retrospective description.

03
HALO Systems Thinking

The engine of dynamics and flow

Hard Infrastructure

Tangible structures, infrastructure, and resource flows. Maps to QAP™ Layer 01: Foundations.

Attitudes

Cultural norms, social trust, and value systems. Maps to QAP™ Layer 05: Paradigms.

Laws & Institutions

Formal rules, governance architecture, and institutional constraints. Maps to QAP™ Layer 02: Mechanisms.

Operating Environment

Geography, external context, regional dynamics, and environmental pressure. Maps to QAP™ Layer 03: Dynamics.

Three-phase methodology

  • Pre-Modelling: define system boundaries, stocks, and encoded norms.
  • Modelling: test feedback loops, non-linearity, and scenario propagation.
  • Post-Modelling: identify leverage points where focused intervention creates disproportionate benefit.

Homeostasis and attractor planes

Systems seek steady states through encoded norms and tolerance thresholds. When those thresholds are breached, systems begin to drift toward attractor planes: either a sustainable peace trajectory or a conflict trap in which violence erodes the very structures needed for recovery.

05
Structured Analytic Tradecraft

Turning frameworks into disciplined judgment

Tradecraft application

  • ACH: identify which evidence truly discriminates between competing trajectories such as sustainable peace versus conflict trap.
  • Key Assumptions Check: surface hidden assumptions embedded in encoded norms and homeostasis expectations.
  • Indicators & Warnings: derive binary triggers from structural weakness and emerging pillar breakdowns.

WSI evidence discipline

All web-sourced material must be evaluated under RAPITIS: reliability, accuracy, primary versus secondary sourcing, independence, timeliness, intent, and specificity. Independence testing is essential to prevent circular sourcing from being mistaken for corroboration.

06
Decision Advantage

The “so what” layer of systemic intelligence

Causal pathway identification

Map how deficits in one structural pillar cascade through the wider system to generate concrete operational risk.

Leverage point identification

Determine which institutional or behavioral nodes can absorb pressure and where focused intervention will matter most.

Shock propagation modeling

Simulate how disruption moves across interdependent variables rather than assuming linear, isolated effects.

Executive checklist

  • What specific decision does the analysis support?
  • What system is being analyzed, and what are its boundaries?
  • Which structural variables are driving the outcome?
  • What is the confidence band for each key judgment?
  • What does the product enable the stakeholder to do: prioritize, mitigate, or anticipate?

Bottom line

Structured systemic intelligence is the most reliable path through complex adaptive environments. It moves analysis beyond reactive reporting and into strategic foresight, where uncertainty is structured, challenged, and converted into decision advantage.

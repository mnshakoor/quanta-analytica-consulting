Cartographic Source Note

This map was produced by the United States Department of State and the National Geographic Society under catalog reference GB201.E3 1996 at a scale of 1:15,600,000. It represents the most comprehensive publicly available single-page cartographic representation of African ethnolinguistic distributions as of its publication date.

Limitations: The map reflects ethnolinguistic distributions as understood in 1996. It does not reflect post-1996 state formation (South Sudan 2011, Eritrea's consolidation), internal displacement events (Darfur 2003–present, DRC Kivu crises, Sahelian displacement), or demographic shifts resulting from urbanization, diaspora formation, or conflict-driven migration. Users should treat this as a baseline cartographic reference, not a current-state assessment. Map Source: United States. Central Intelligence Agency., Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons.

05 // Conflict Relevance Index QAP I&W integration — 13 active ethnolinguistic fault lines
This index identifies ethnolinguistic groups and zones where identity, marginalization, or cross-border distribution constitutes a material conflict driver or amplifier. Entries are drawn from the QAP-CSRF™ fault line taxonomy and cross-referenced against ACLED, UNOCHA, and open-source threat actor databases. Entries are not exhaustive; they represent the highest-priority analytical reference points derived from the 1996 cartographic dataset.
